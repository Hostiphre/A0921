Ab initio calculation of weak interaction
Post-doc project about method development based on VASP, started from Sept. 4 2017. MFF, Charles University in Prague.

Weak interactions (such as van der Waals interaction) concern competitions within small energy differences (meV), therefore require high accuracy of calculation. The systems to be studied in this post-doc are those where weak interactions are important, or those where there is a competition between strong chemical and weak physical forces. Although there already exist various kinds of methods and corrections, some times highly accurate methods are still required to produce benchmark values. The objective is to understand the limit of different methods/approximations in order to give truly productive method which can be faster than the current benchmark method CCSD(T). The developed method will be integrated in VASP.
Molecular Density Functional Theory
PhD thesis at Maison de la Simulation, CEA Saclay, France.

Molecular Density Functional Theory (MDFT) is a very prospective (but not yet mature) method to treat solvation interactions, much better than continuum models. This work reaches an investigation of the full 3-angle Homogenous Reference Fluid (HRF) approximation, second order Taylor expansion of the excess Helmholtz free energy, which gives the possibility to investigate higher order multi-body terms. The calculation under HRF approximation is accelerated with no precision lost, by taking advantage of the translational and rotational invariance which leads to a much simplified OZ equation. This idea is proposed by my professor Luc Belloni, based on the mathematical equivalent chi-transform proposed by L.Blum for Integral Equation Theory (IET). My work is to detail and implement the theory into the code MDFT developed by my professor Daniel Borgis and Maximien Levesque. There are several similar works which reach to describe the 3D solvent density in Generalised Spherical Harmonics (GSH) projections, but no one else take into account the chi-transform, i.e. rotation to the local k-frame, which is mandatory for the simplification of OZ equation, as shown in my poster 2e section: http://cyanland.net/temp/A0731.pdf

For details, c.f. my thesis at http://cyanland.net/temp/M1116.pdf
